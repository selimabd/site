My story

Mr. Haysam Selim is a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science, College of Engineering, University of Nevada, Las Vegas. His current research work is in big data analytics, machine learning and deep learning. He has received the Master of Science in Computer Science from North Carolina A&T State University in May 2015. His MS research area is in Network Theory and Graph Theory as well as big data analytics. He received a Bachelor of Computer Science in Network Security from American University in the Emirates (AUE). He is the Lab Manager of Big Data Hub – ILAB at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas, where he is conducting research on Deep Learning for Big Data Analytics. Prior to this, he was an Assistant Research Manager of ILAB in North Carolina A&T State University from 2013 to 2015 where he conducted research and experiments for the following projects funded by Department of Defense (DOD) and National Science Foundation (NSF): 1) Social Network Analysis and Simulation (SONAS) system – the objective to build a social network analysis and simulation system through extensively study different social networks; and 2) A Novel Framework for Community Detection in Large Networks (DoD) – the objective to invent better models and community detection methods through extensive study of different networked systems using game-theoretic models, network entropy spectral metrics, and eigenvalue analysis. He also managed Big Data Storage Cluster of a Storage capacity of 1PB (Petabyte) as well as managed Big Data Analytic Cluster.
